Instructions

The figure given below shows a simulated climate change stabilization task that you will be playing today for a period of 100 years (where years can be read from information panel circled in red). The task shows a tank with an orange liquid which represents the CO2 concentration in the atmosphere. The CO2 concentration can be read from the blue box with labe "Co2 Concentration" or graphical panel circled in grey (where the green line shows the trajectory of CO2 concentration). The grey circled graphics also shows goals for CO2 concentration to be attained in the future (ie the green trajectory). Also, the graphics circled in brown shows the trajectory of total CO2 emissions and CO2 absorptions. Pipes labeled "Total Emissions" and "Absorptions" with corresponding black blips showing numerical values in pink box represent the total CO2 added and removed from the atmosphere each year You start in the year 2000 when the CO2 concentration in the atmosphere is 765 GTC (or billion tons of carbon) and total emissions equal 8.155 GtC/year. In year 2000. the value of CO2 absorptions was half of the total CO2 emissions and equaled 4.077 GtC/year.
